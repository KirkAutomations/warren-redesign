Biography

Judge Michael Warren

Judge Michael Warren has served the people of Oakland County, Michigan, from the Circuit Court bench for over twenty years. During his distinguished tenure, he has presided over more than 360 jury trials — spanning over 1,000 days of trial proceedings. This extraordinary record gives him more trial experience than the entire United States Supreme Court combined.

A graduate of the University of Michigan Law School, where he earned his Juris Doctor cum laude, Judge Warren also holds an Honors History Degree from Wayne State University. This dual foundation in law and American history informs every aspect of his judicial career and his broader work in civic education.

Judge Warren's dedication to the legal profession and public service has been widely recognized. The International Association of Top Professionals named him Judge of the Decade and inducted him into their prestigious Hall of Fame. The Oakland County Bar Association honored him with the Distinguished Public Servant Award — a testament to his impact both on and off the bench.

Beyond the courtroom, Judge Warren serves as a Constitutional Law Professor at Western Michigan University Thomas M. Cooley Law School, where he shapes the next generation of legal minds. He has also served as a member of the Michigan State Board of Education, bringing his commitment to civic literacy to the state's educational policy.

Judge Warren's passion for the Constitution extends into public life through his work as co-founder of Patriot Week (alongside his daughter Leah), author of "America's Survival Guide," and host of the Patriot Lessons podcast and television show. His mission: to ensure that every American understands the First Principles and founding documents that sustain our republic.

On the bench, Judge Warren is known for interpreting the law as written — he does not legislate from the bench. He is also the creator of the innovative Reset Program, designed to help probationers find meaning and purpose in their lives, reflecting his belief that justice includes the opportunity for redemption.

Judicial Philosophy

The Law as Written

"A judge's role is to interpret the law, not to make it. The Constitution means what it says — and what it says is the supreme law of the land."

Judge Warren's approach to the bench is grounded in constitutional originalism and judicial restraint. He believes that judges must apply the law as written by the legislature and as intended by the framers of the Constitution, not substitute their own policy preferences.

This philosophy extends to his innovative Reset Program, which helps probationers find meaning and direction in their lives. For Judge Warren, justice is not merely punitive — it includes the opportunity for individuals to understand their obligations, embrace responsibility, and build a purposeful life.
